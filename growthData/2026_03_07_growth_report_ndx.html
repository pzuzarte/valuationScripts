📖 Glossary

Scoring Pillars
Growth ScoreComposite 0–100 rating combining four pillars: Quality (0–35), Growth Momentum (0–35), Technical Signal (0–20), and Valuation (0–10). Each risk flag deducts 4 points.
Quality Pillar0–35 pts. Measures business durability via ROIC (10), ROE (4), gross margin (8), operating margin (5), FCF generation (5), and liquidity/current ratio (3).
Growth Momentum0–35 pts. Multi-axis growth assessment: revenue growth + QoQ acceleration (10), EPS growth + acceleration (8), gross profit growth (4), FCF growth (5), net income growth (4), EBITDA growth (3), and R&D investment (3).
Technical Signal0–20 pts. Market confirmation of fundamentals: TV composite rating (4), MoneyFlow (4), RSI (2), Stochastic (2), Williams %R (1), price performance across 1M/3M/6M/YTD (7), and 52-week position (1).
Valuation Pillar0–10 pts. Multi-lens value check: upside to model target (4), forward PEG (2), P/FCF (2), and EV/EBITDA (2).
Price Target Methods
Forward PEGPrice Target = Forward EPS × min(Forward Growth% × PEG target, 80x). Three scenarios: Bear (PEG 0.75x), Base (1.0x), Bull (1.5x). Weight: 60% of composite.
Revenue Extrap.Projects forward revenue × net margin × growth-adjusted sector P/E ÷ shares. Uses analyst consensus revenue when available. Weight: 40% of composite.
EV/EBITDAFair value = (Forward EBITDA × growth-adjusted sector multiple) − net debt, divided by shares. Captures capital-structure-neutral value. Weight: 20% of composite.
Composite TargetWeighted average of all available methods, renormalised if a method has insufficient data. Bear/bull targets use ±20% on revenue and EV methods.
Forward EPSPriority: (1) Analyst next-FY consensus, (2) analyst next-quarter × 4 annualised, (3) TTM EPS × blended growth rate, (4) last fiscal year EPS × growth.
Forward GrowthImplied from analyst forward EPS vs TTM when available. Otherwise blended: 70% quarterly + 30% TTM if accelerating, 50/50 if decelerating. Capped at 80%.

Below EMA 13Short-term pullback — stock is dipping below its 13-day EMA. Often a brief retracement in an uptrend.
Below EMA 50Medium-term weakness — stock has broken below its 50-day EMA. May indicate a deeper correction or trend shift.
Below EMA 200Long-term downtrend territory — stock is trading below its 200-day EMA. Widely watched as a bull/bear dividing line.
Below EMA 13 & 50Stock is below both short and medium-term EMAs. Confirms the pullback has more substance than a one-day dip.
Below EMA 13, 50 & 200Deepest pullback — stock is below all three EMAs. Maximum discount but also maximum trend risk. Best combined with high Growth Score and clean flags.
RSI FilterFilters stocks by RSI(14) zone. Use the dropdown to isolate oversold dip-buy candidates or avoid overbought names.
Oversold (RSI < 30)Classic oversold territory. Stock has seen heavy selling and may be due for a bounce. Strongest contrarian signal.
Oversold–Weak (RSI < 40)Broader oversold zone including stocks with bearish momentum that haven’t yet reached extreme levels. Wider net for dip candidates.
Neutral (RSI 40–60)Neither overbought nor oversold. Stock is in a consolidation or steady-trend phase with no extreme momentum signal.
Strong–Overbought (RSI > 60)Stock has strong upward momentum. May continue trending higher but risk of a pullback increases as RSI rises.
Overbought (RSI > 70)Classic overbought territory. Price may be extended and vulnerable to a correction or consolidation.
Extreme Overbought (RSI > 80)Extremely overbought. Rare and usually unsustainable. High risk of a sharp pullback in the near term.

Common Abbreviations
TTMTrailing Twelve Months. Sum of the last four reported quarters.
FYFiscal Year. A company’s full annual reporting period (may not align with calendar year).
FQFiscal Quarter. One quarter of the fiscal year.
YoYYear-over-Year. Compares a metric to the same period one year ago.
QoQQuarter-over-Quarter. Compares a metric to the immediately prior quarter (sequential growth).
EPSEarnings Per Share. Net income ÷ diluted shares outstanding.
FCFFree Cash Flow. Operating cash flow − capital expenditures. Cash available for dividends, buybacks, or reinvestment.
EBITDAEarnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortisation. Proxy for operating cash generation.
EVEnterprise Value. Market cap + total debt − cash. Represents the total cost to acquire a business.
EMAExponential Moving Average. A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices. Faster to react to price changes than a simple moving average (SMA).
P/EPrice-to-Earnings ratio. Share price ÷ EPS. Higher = market expects more growth.
PEGPrice/Earnings-to-Growth. P/E ÷ earnings growth rate. Normalises valuation for growth speed. <1.0 considered attractive.
COGSCost of Goods Sold. Direct costs attributable to producing goods/services sold.
GICSGlobal Industry Classification Standard. The sector/industry taxonomy used by S&P and MSCI.
TVTradingView. The financial data platform used as the data source for this screener.
MRQMost Recent Quarter. The latest reported fiscal quarter.
R&DResearch & Development. Spending on innovation and future products/services.
D/EDebt-to-Equity ratio. Total debt ÷ shareholders’ equity. Measures financial leverage.
